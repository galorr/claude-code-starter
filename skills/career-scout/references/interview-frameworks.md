## Universal Questions — Asked in Almost Every First Interview

| Question | What they're really testing |
|---|---|
| "Tell me about yourself" | Can you narrate your career story in 3 minutes? Does it make sense? |
| "Why are you leaving [current company]?" | Are you running away (bad) or running toward something (good)? |
| "Why us / why this company?" | Did you do homework? Are you genuinely interested? |
| "What are you looking for in your next role?" | Are your goals aligned with what they can offer? |
| "Tell me about a time something went wrong" | Are you honest? Can you own failure and learn? |
| "Where do you see yourself in 3 years?" | Ambition, realism, and likely tenure |
| "What are your salary expectations?" | Budget alignment — answer with a researched range |

## STAR Story Framework

For every behavioral question, use this structure:

**S — Situation** (20% of answer time)
Set the scene briefly. Context: company stage, team size, the constraint.

**T — Task** (10% of answer time)
Your specific responsibility. What were YOU accountable for?

**A — Action** (50% of answer time)
What you specifically did — 3-4 concrete steps. Use "I", not "we".
Include: who you spoke with, what you changed, how you overcame resistance.

**R — Result** (20% of answer time)
Quantified outcome. Always: %, $, x improvement, or timeline saved.
Optional: what you learned / what you'd do differently.

## Question Banks by Profession

Pick the bank matching the candidate's profession. Each question lists what the
interviewer is really testing.

### Software Engineering (IC)
- "Walk me through a hard technical problem you solved" → depth, problem-solving, ownership
- "How do you approach code review?" → collaboration, quality bar
- "Tell me about a system you designed" → architecture thinking (scale to seniority)
- "How do you handle a production incident?" → calm under pressure, debugging method
- "How do you keep your skills current?" → growth mindset
- Live coding / system design exercise → expect this for mid+ levels

### Engineering Management / Leadership
- "What's the largest team you've managed?" → can you operate at the needed scale
- "How do you handle an underperformer?" → EQ + courage; expect PIP discussion
- "How do you improve team velocity?" → real metrics and methods, not theory
- "How do you align engineering with business goals?" → business partnership
- "What AI/productivity tools have you introduced?" → 2025+ this is almost always asked
- "Design the org structure for a 30-person group" → architectural thinking about teams

### Product Management
- "How do you prioritize the roadmap?" → framework + judgment (RICE, value vs. effort)
- "Tell me about a product you shipped and its impact" → outcomes, not output
- "How do you say no to a stakeholder?" → influence without authority
- "How do you work with engineering and design?" → collaboration model
- "How do you use data vs. intuition?" → balanced decision-making
- "A feature failed after launch — what did you do?" → learning loop

### Design (UX / UI / Product)
- "Walk me through your portfolio / a project end to end" → process, not just visuals
- "How do you handle critique and conflicting feedback?" → maturity, collaboration
- "How do you balance user needs with business constraints?" → pragmatism
- "How do you work with PMs and engineers?" → cross-functional fit
- "How do you measure if a design succeeded?" → outcome orientation
- A design exercise / whiteboard challenge → expect for most levels

### Marketing
- "Walk me through a campaign you ran and its results" → ownership + numbers
- "Which channels do you know best and why?" → depth vs. breadth
- "How do you measure marketing ROI?" → analytical rigor
- "How do you work with sales / product?" → alignment
- "Tell me about a campaign that underperformed" → honesty + iteration
- "How do you approach brand vs. demand gen?" → strategic balance

### Sales
- "Walk me through your sales process" → method and discipline
- "Tell me about your biggest deal" → hunting ability, deal size
- "How do you handle a long stalled deal?" → persistence + qualification
- "What's your approach to a quota you're behind on?" → resilience, pipeline math
- "How do you research a prospect?" → preparation
- "Why this product / market?" → genuine interest

### Data / Analytics / Data Science
- "Walk me through an analysis that changed a decision" → business impact
- "How do you validate a model / a result?" → rigor
- "How do you explain technical findings to non-technical stakeholders?" → communication
- "What's your tooling and why?" → practical depth
- A SQL / case / take-home exercise → expect this

### Finance / Operations / HR / Legal / Other
- "Tell me about a process you improved" → impact + initiative
- "How do you handle competing priorities?" → organization
- "Describe a difficult stakeholder situation" → diplomacy
- "What's the scale you've operated at?" → fit to company size
- "Why this company / industry?" → genuine interest

## Question Modifiers by Seniority

Layer these onto the profession bank.

**Junior / Early-career:** more on fundamentals, learning ability, and attitude.
Expect "Tell me about a time you learned something fast" and skills tests.

**Mid-level:** more on independence and ownership.
"Tell me about a project you drove end to end with little supervision."

**Senior / Lead:** more on influence, mentoring, and judgment.
"How do you raise the bar for people around you?"

**Manager / Director / VP / C-level:** more on people, strategy, and business.
"How do you build and structure a team?" "How do you align your function with the business?"

## Questions TO ASK the Interviewer

### For your direct future manager:
1. **"What does success look like for this role at 6 months and at 1 year?"**
   Why: Shows maturity. Reveals real expectations. Red flag: "We'll figure it out."
2. **"What's the biggest challenge this team / function is facing right now?"**
   Why: Honest conversation. Reveals what you're inheriting. Red flag: "Everything's fine."
3. **"What happened with the previous person in this role?"**
   Why: Most revealing question. Red flag: vagueness or deflection.
4. **"How is success measured here, and how often is feedback given?"**
   Why: Reveals the performance culture.

### For a cross-functional / stakeholder interviewer:
1. "How does your team and this role work together day to day?"
2. "What's the biggest thing slowing your team down right now?"
3. "What did the previous person in this role do well, and what would you change?"
4. "How are priorities decided across functions here?"

### For an HR / recruiter screen:
1. "What does the rest of the interview process look like?"
2. "How would you describe the culture in one honest sentence?"
3. "What's the typical career path for this role here?"

## Strong Closing Lines (customize per company)

### Template:
"What drew me to [COMPANY] is [SPECIFIC REASON tied to their product/stage/growth].
My background in [RELEVANT EXPERIENCE] means I can [SPECIFIC VALUE] from day one.
I'm genuinely excited about this opportunity."

### Example — IC role at a growth-stage company:
"What drew me to [COMPANY] is that you're at the stage where one person's work is
still clearly visible in the product. My experience [specific skill + result] maps
directly to what this role needs. I'd love to be part of this."

### Example — leadership role at a pre-IPO company:
"What drew me to [COMPANY] is the pre-IPO stage combined with [X]% growth — the kind of
trajectory where a leader can shape the function, not just maintain it. My background in
[relevant experience and a quantified result] is exactly what a company at your stage needs."

## Interviewer Research Signals

When researching the interviewer, look for these signals and adapt your prep:

| Signal | What it means for your prep |
|---|---|
| Long enterprise background | They value process, quality, rigor |
| Startup-only background | They value speed, bias to action, scrappiness |
| MBA | They think in business terms — use ROI language |
| Ex-military | They respect structure, discipline, mission |
| Published posts / talks | Read them — it reveals their philosophy |
| "Servant leader" language | They want autonomy, not micromanagement |
| Deep technical title (CTO, Principal) | Technical depth matters — know your craft |
| People-leader title (VP, Director) | People + process + delivery all matter |
| Came up through the same profession as you | They'll go deep on craft — be specific |
