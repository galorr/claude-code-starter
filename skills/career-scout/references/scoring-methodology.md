# Scoring Methodology — Company Ranking

This rubric works for any profession and any seniority. The target position always
comes from the candidate's CV and their stated goals — never assumed.

## The Four Criteria

### 1. Growth & Momentum (30% weight)

| Score | Signals |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | 40%+ YoY revenue growth, recent large funding (last 6 months), aggressive hiring |
| 7–8 | 20–40% YoY growth, healthy funding, stable headcount growth |
| 5–6 | 10–20% growth, profitable but slow, or early-stage momentum |
| 3–4 | <10% growth, flat or declining revenue, recent downsizing |
| 1–2 | Declining revenue, multiple layoff rounds, financial distress |

### 2. Equity Potential (25% weight)

| Score | Signals |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Pre-IPO with strong growth ($500M–$5B val), IPO rumored within 2 years, secondary already paid |
| 7–8 | Public with rising stock, or late-stage private with clear exit path, top-tier VC backing |
| 5–6 | Public with flat stock, or PE-backed with 3–5 year exit timeline |
| 3–4 | Public with declining stock (-50%+), or early-stage with uncertain exit |
| 1–2 | Public stock down 80%+, debt financing only, or no funding since 2021 |

Note: weight this lower in the candidate's mental model if their stated stage preference
is "public company (stability)" or if they are early-career and equity is not a priority.

### 3. Role Fit (25% weight)

Match between the candidate's profession, seniority, skills, and domain and what this
company needs. **This criterion adapts to whatever role the CV indicates.**

| Score | Signals |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Company actively hires this exact role + same domain; candidate's skills/seniority map directly |
| 7–8 | Same role family OR same domain — minor gap easily bridged |
| 5–6 | Different domain but clearly transferable skills, or a small seniority stretch |
| 3–4 | Significant domain gap, or a meaningful seniority mismatch (over- or under-qualified) |
| 1–2 | Company has no roles in this profession, or no transferable overlap at all |

**How to assess Role Fit — adapt the lens to the candidate's profession:**
- *Engineering* — tech stack overlap, scale of systems, domain (SaaS, FinTech, security, etc.)
- *Product* — product type (B2B/B2C/PLG), user base, stage of product maturity
- *Design* — design discipline (UX, UI, product, brand), tooling, portfolio relevance
- *Marketing* — channels and motions (PLG, demand gen, brand, content), audience type
- *Sales* — deal size, sales motion (SMB/mid-market/enterprise), industry
- *Data / Analytics* — data maturity, tooling, whether the role is BI, DS, or DE
- *Finance / Ops / HR / Legal* — company stage and complexity vs. the candidate's experience
- *Seniority* — does the company have a role at the candidate's level, or is it a stretch
  up (good if intentional) or a step down (flag it)?

### 4. Culture (20% weight)

| Score | Signals |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | 4.5+ Glassdoor (candidate's region), 90%+ recommendation, zero layoffs in 3 years |
| 7–8 | 4.0–4.5 Glassdoor, 80%+ recommendation, stable leadership |
| 5–6 | 3.5–4.0 Glassdoor, mixed reviews, one layoff round |
| 3–4 | 3.0–3.5 Glassdoor, frequent leadership changes, multiple layoffs |
| 1–2 | <3.0 Glassdoor, toxic culture reports, 3+ layoff rounds |

Use the candidate's country/office-specific Glassdoor score when available — culture
varies a lot by site.

## Weighted Score Formula

```
Final Score = (Growth × 0.30) + (Equity × 0.25) + (Role Fit × 0.25) + (Culture × 0.20)
```

## Automatic Score Caps

**Cap at 5/10 if any of these are true:**
- 3+ layoff rounds in 3 years
- Stock down 80%+ from peak
- Revenue growth under 5% for 2 consecutive years
- Glassdoor below 3.0 globally
- C-suite mass exodus: 3+ C-level departures in one year

## Green Flags — Always Mention in Report

- Pre-IPO with top-tier VC backing (Sequoia, Insight, Tiger Global, a16z, etc.)
- Glassdoor 4.5+ specifically in the candidate's region/office
- Recent large funding round (last 6 months)
- The company is actively and visibly hiring for the candidate's exact role
- Local office expanding aggressively (doubling headcount plans)
- Secondary transaction paid to employees (de-risks equity)
- No layoffs in 5+ years
- A clear career path for the candidate's profession inside the org

## Calibration Examples (engineering-leadership sample — adapt the Role Fit lens per candidate)

| Company | Growth | Equity | Role Fit | Culture | Final |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strong pre-IPO, fast growth, same domain | 9 | 10 | 7 | 8 | 9.5/10 |
| Public, stable, exact role + domain match | 8 | 8 | 10 | 9 | 9.0/10 |
| Public DevOps leader, partial domain match | 8 | 8 | 7 | 9 | 8.5/10 |
| Hyper-growth startup, adjacent domain | 10 | 9 | 5 | 6 | 7.5/10 |
| Declining public co, role fits but distress | 2 | 1 | 6 | 4 | 3.5/10 |
| Mid-growth, debt financing, weak culture | 6 | 4 | 5 | 3 | 5.5/10 |

These numbers illustrate the formula. The Role Fit column must always be recomputed
for the actual candidate's profession and seniority.
